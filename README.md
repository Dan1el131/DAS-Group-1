## 3.1 Modelling weight/obesity in Scotland

The weight and categorisation of weight (namely obesity) in Scotland has been monitored since the introduction of the Scottish Health Survey, which is designed to monitor the health of the Scottish population living in private households. The main aim of the survey is to keep an eye on health trends in Scotland. The Scottish Heath Survey data will be used to explore trends in weight/obesity in Scotland.

---

## Project 1 — Obesity prevalence

Data are available on socio-economic and lifestyle factors from the **2008–2012 Scottish Health Surveys**.  
The data are stored in **`DAProject1.csv`** and contain the following columns.

### Variables

- **Age** — Age of individual  
- **Sex** — Sex of individual (Male / Female)  
- **Education** — Highest educational qualification of individual  
- **Veg** — Consume recommended daily vegetable intake (Yes / No)  
- **Fruit** — Consume recommended daily fruit intake (Yes / No)  
- **Year** — Year of the Scottish Health Survey  
- **Obese** — Indicator of individuals obesity classification (Yes / No)

---

## Questions of Interest

The main questions of interest are:

1. **Has the prevalence of obesity in Scotland changed over the given years of the Scottish Health Survey?**

2. **Are there any differences in obesity by age, gender, socio-economic status or lifestyle factors?**
